Balut

Fertilized egg commonly sold as street food in the Philippines

A balut is a fertilized bird egg, usually duck, which is incubated for a period of 14 to 21 days and then steamed. The contents are eaten directly from the shell. The partially developed embryo bones are soft enough to chew and swallow as a whole. A combination of savory, gamey and rich characterisitics can be found in the flavor which makes it an acquired taste that many Philipinos treasure as a culinary treat and a part of their culture.

Ingredients

  • Fertilized egg of choice
  • salt, chili, garlic, vinegar for taste

Steps

  1. Incubate egg in sun or buried in sand
  2. After nine days hold to light to examine embryo.
  3. The duration is matter of local preference, typically 14 to 18 days
  4. boil in water
  5. serve with salt, chili, garlic and vinegar
